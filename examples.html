Apple Watches and Fitbits

Apple Watch Fitbit
Where is this technology being used?

The Apple Watch is globally used in everyday environments such as at home, the gym, and work. Furthermore, according to npj Digital Medicine, it is used in clinical and research settings where patient data is monitored remotely.

 Fitbit
Who is using it?
  • General Consumers such as students, parents, etc. (for fitness tracking, wellness)
  • Patients with health concerns (e.g. heart rhythm monitoring)
  • Healthcare providers and researchers
 Fitbit
What is the purpose of using it?
  • Tracks physical activity, heart rate, sleep, and other health metrics
  • Detects irregular health patterns (e.g. abnormal heart rhythms)
  • Support preventative healthcare and early intervention
 Fitbit
What benefits does it provide?
  • Enables users to be more aware of their health behaviors and patterns
  • Encourage physical activity and healthier habits
  • Provides real-time feedback (heart rate, exercise intensity)
  • Can alert users to potential health issues
  • Convenient as you can message and call your contacts without iPhone
 Fitbit
What problems or risks does it create?
  • Accuracy: some metrics are estimates and may be unreliable
  • Dependency: users may treat it as a medical device when it is not, or obsess over metrics, leading to anxiety
  • Privacy: sensitive health data is collected and stored, potential data leaks
 Fitbit
What makes this technology difficult to use responsibly in this context?
  • Users may misinterpret data / use data over medical advice
  • Lack of clear regulation (wellness vs. medical device boundary)
  • Accuracy: some metrics are estimates and may be unreliable
